Sooji Halwa

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup sooji (rava, semolina flour)
  • 1/4 cup ghee (clear butter)
  • 1/3 cup sugar
  • 1 1/2cup water
  • Pinch of cardamom powder
  • 1 tablespoon sliced almonds for garnish

Method:

Boil the water with sugar, and cardamom powder in a pan over low medium heat. Bring to a boil and Set aside.
Melt the butter in a frying pan on medium heat.
Add sooji and roast to golden brown color; stirring constantly this will take about 5 to 8 minutes. Sooji will be golden brown in color and will have a light sweet aroma.
Add the syrup slowly, as you add the syrup to sooji water will splatter.
Turn down the heat to medium low and let it cook for about 2-3 minutes.
Sooji will absorb the water, as the Halwa cools so keep Halwa a little more softer then you want.
Garnish with almonds.

Gajar ka Halwa

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg carrots
  • 1 1/2 litre milk
  • 8 green cardamoms
  • 5-7 Tbsp ghee
  • 5-7 Tbsp sugar
  • 2 Tbsp raisins
  • 1 Tbsp shredded almonds
  • 2 Tbsp chopped dates

Method:

Peel and grate the carrots.
Simmer in milk with the cardamom until liquid evaporates.
Heat ghee in a heavy pan and add the carrot mixture.
Cook over a gentle flame for 10-15 minutes.
Stir in sugar and continue cooking until the halwa turns deep reddish color.
Stir in dried fruit and serve.

Veg Pizza

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup grated mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup sliced and blanched baby corn
  • 1/2 cup blanched carrot cubes
  • 1/2 cup blanched zucchini cubes
  • 1/2 cup sliced red capsicum
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • salt and to taste
  • 1/2 cup pizza sauce

Method:

Heat the butter in a broad non-stick pan, add all the vegetables and sauté on a medium flame for 2 to 3 minutes.
Add the salt and pepper and mix well.
Place a pizza base on a clean, dry surface and spread some portion of the vegetable topping evenly over it.
Sprinkle ¼ cup of cheese over it.
Place the pizza on a greased baking tray and bake in a pre-heated oven at 200°c (400°f) for 10 to 12 minutes or till the base is evenly browned and the cheese melts.
Cut into equal wedges and serve immediately.

Non Veg Pizza

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup grated mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup sliced and blanched baby corn
  • 1/2 cup blanched carrot cubes
  • 1/2 cup blanched zucchini cubes
  • 1/2 cup sliced red capsicum
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • salt and to taste
  • fried chicken
  • 1/2 cup pizza sauce

Method:

Heat the butter in a broad non-stick pan, add all the vegetables and sauté on a medium flame for 2 to 3 minutes.
Add the salt and pepper and mix well.
Place a pizza base on a clean, dry surface and spread ¼ cup of pizza sauce and some portion of the vegetable topping evenly over it.
Grate the fried chicken and spread some portion of chicken over it
Finally sprinkle ¼ cup of cheese over it.
Place the pizza on a greased baking tray and bake in a pre-heated oven at 200°c (400°f) for 10 to 12 minutes or till the base is evenly browned and the cheese melts.
Cut into equal wedges and serve immediately.

Plain Pancake

Ingredients:

  • all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp. sugar
  • 2 1/2 tsp. baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp. salt
  • 1 1/4 c. milk
  • 3 tbsp. butter, melted
  • 1 large egg
  • Vegetable oil for brushing pan

Method:

In large bowl, whisk flour, sugar, baking powder and salt. Add milk, butter and egg; stir until flour is moistened.
Heat 12-inch nonstick skillet or griddle over medium heat until drop of water sizzles; brush lightly with oil. In batches, scoop batter by scant 1/4-cupfuls into skillet, spreading to 3 1/2 inches each. Cook 2 to 3 minutes or until bubbly and edges are dry. With wide spatula, turn; cook 2 minutes more or until golden. Transfer to platter or keep warm on a cookie sheet in 225°F oven.
Repeat with remaining batter, brushing griddle with more oil if necessary.

Banana Pancake

Ingredients:

  • all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp. sugar
  • 2 1/2 tsp. baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp. salt
  • 1 1/4 c. milk
  • 1 1/2 cup mashed banana
  • 3 tbsp. butter, melted
  • 1 large egg
  • Vegetable oil for brushing pan

Method:

In large bowl, whisk flour, sugar, baking powder, mashed banana and salt. Add milk, butter and egg; stir until flour is moistened.
Heat 12-inch nonstick skillet or griddle over medium heat until drop of water sizzles; brush lightly with oil. In batches, scoop batter by scant 1/4-cupfuls into skillet, spreading to 3 1/2 inches each. Cook 2 to 3 minutes or until bubbly and edges are dry. With wide spatula, turn; cook 2 minutes more or until golden. Transfer to platter or keep warm on a cookie sheet in 225°F oven.
Repeat with remaining batter, brushing griddle with more oil if necessary.
